Part C: Short Answer

Difference Between Semantic and Generic Elements

A semantic element such as <nav> clearly describes its meaning and purpose to both the browser and assistive technologies, improving accessibility and structure. A generic element such as <div> has no inherent meaning and is used only for grouping content or styling.

Why KLM Does Not Model High-Level Planning and Interpretation

The Keystroke-Level Model (KLM) focuses on predicting the execution time of routine, expert, and error-free physical actions. It does not model high-level planning, perception, or interpretation because these cognitive processes are abstract and vary between users, making them difficult to quantify precisely.
