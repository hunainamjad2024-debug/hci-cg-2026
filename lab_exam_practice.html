Part C: Short Answer

Difference Between Semantic and Generic Elements

A semantic element such as <nav> clearly describes its purpose and meaning to browsers and assistive technologies, improving accessibility and structure. A generic element such as <div> has no built-in meaning and is used only to group or style content.

Why KLM Does Not Model High-Level Planning and Interpretation

The Keystroke-Level Model (KLM) predicts the execution time of routine, expert, error-free physical actions. It does not include high-level planning or interpretation because these cognitive processes vary between users and are difficult to measure precisely.
